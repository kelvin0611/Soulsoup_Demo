Hong Kong’s Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) clinics face major barriers to digital transformation. Our preliminary research indicates that over 80% of local clinics still rely on paper-based records and manual appointment booking, causing operational inefficiencies, fragmented patient management, and missed follow-up opportunities. At the same time, urban citizens with “sub-health” conditions, such as insomnia, constipation, chronic fatigue and stress-related symptoms, which is lack personalised, data-informed wellness solutions aligned with their TCM body constitution. Most existing wellness products are “one-size-fits-all” and do not differentiate among TCM body types (e.g. damp-heat, qi deficiency, yin deficiency).

Our project, “TCM ClinicLink”, addresses these dual pain points through an integrated B2B2C digital health platform that connects professional TCM services with everyday preventive care.

For clinics: We are developing an AI-driven clinic management system with three core modules.
1.	An intelligent appointment scheduler with automated reminders to reduce no-shows and optimise clinic capacity.
2.	Electronic health records (EHR) with OCR digitisation of handwritten notes to enable longitudinal patient tracking, structured data collection, and analytics.
3.	An NLP-based classical text retrieval engine that lets practitioners input symptoms and instantly access relevant passages from authoritative TCM texts such as Shang Han Lun and Jin Gui Yao Lue. This supports junior clinicians’ clinical reasoning and research while preserving the practitioner’s final diagnostic judgment.

For patients: Based on the TCM practitioner’s diagnosis, the system analyses body constitution and generates recommendations for personalised “medicine-food homologue” soup packs. These packs use ingredients recognised in Chinese dietary therapy and legally classified as food, reducing regulatory barriers while aligning with TCM food therapy principles. Patients scan a QR code at the clinic to access tailored recommendations and place orders for home delivery via partnered logistics providers. Clinics receive a revenue share from each order, creating an ongoing incentive to adopt and promote the platform.

Innovation: SoulSoup is positioned as Hong Kong’s first platform that systematically bridges TCM clinical practice with daily, personalised preventive care using AI (NLP, OCR, and machine learning–based constitution analysis). Our dual-sided solution enhances clinic digitalisation and patient engagement while enabling a recurring, data-driven revenue model.

Market potential: Hong Kong has around 1,700 TCM clinics, including 18 government-funded district clinics and numerous private practices. With an estimated 70% of adults experiencing sub-health conditions, there is strong demand for personalised, TCM-aligned wellness solutions. Our business model combines monthly SaaS subscription fees from clinics (Phase 1) with revenue sharing from soup pack sales (Phase 2), targeting HK$2 million in annual revenue by Year 3.

Team and impact: Our interdisciplinary team includes Computer Science students (AI/NLP and system development), a Fintech student (business model, market and financial analysis), and a UI/UX designer (product interface and user experience). We have self-studied TCM fundamentals and plan to work with an industry advisor or academic consultant to ensure clinical accuracy and cultural appropriateness. By digitalising TCM practice and enabling personalised preventive healthcare, SoulSoup supports clinic efficiency, nurtures young TCM practitioners, and aligns with HKSAR policy directions on TCM development and healthcare burden reduction.
